## The problem

Most DIY voice assistants are slow. Between finishing speaking and hearing the robot's first word, 2 to 4 seconds pass. Above 1.5 seconds it feels robotic. Above 3, the user gives up and the illusion of conversation breaks entirely.

The second problem is equally serious: existing projects aren't optimized for Latin American Spanish. STT models struggle with regional accents, voseo conjugations, and local vocabulary. TTS voices sound artificial — neutral dubbing accent, no natural cadence. The result is an experience that feels foreign in your own language.

## The solution

Michibot has one non-negotiable goal: the robot's first word must be audible in under 900ms for complex turns, and under 200ms for known responses. At that speed, conversation stops feeling like talking to a machine and starts feeling like a real exchange.

The architecture uses end-to-end streaming from day one — no stage waits for the previous one to complete. The pipeline flows: on-device wake word on the ESP32-S3, partial transcription via Deepgram, intelligent 3-tier routing, and ElevenLabs voice synthesis that starts generating while the LLM is still producing tokens.

The hardware combines ElectronBot's body (servos for expressiveness + display for visual context) with the ESP32-S3's audio capabilities, all coordinated by a Python/FastAPI backend acting as the conversational brain.

## Stack and decisions

**FastAPI + async Python** as backend: handling WebSockets and audio streaming required a genuinely asynchronous server. Each pipeline stage (STT → Router → LLM → TTS) runs in overlapping parallel — TTS starts generating on the LLM's first tokens, not once the full sentence is complete.

**ESP32-S3** for on-device wake word: initial audio processing happens on the microcontroller without sending anything to the server. This eliminates network latency on the detection step and protects user privacy — audio is only transmitted once the activation word is detected locally.

**3-tier router**: Tier 1 covers 70% of turns with predefined responses in under 200ms. Tier 2 uses parameterized templates for semi-dynamic responses at ~400ms. Only Tier 3 calls the full LLM, used on fewer than 30% of turns. This cuts API cost by ~70% and keeps perceived latency consistently low.

**Deepgram** for STT, chosen for its partial streaming support and superior performance on LATAM Spanish compared to Whisper in production. **ElevenLabs** for TTS, for the naturalness of its voice in Rioplatense Spanish.

## Impact

- First-audio under 900ms on complex turns (p50 measured)
- 70% of turns resolved without a single LLM call
- API cost dramatically reduced thanks to the tier system
- Open source, documented in Spanish, with architecture replicable for other languages
- Real proof of concept: embedded hardware + low-latency conversational AI working in the physical world
