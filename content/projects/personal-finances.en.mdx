## The problem

Existing personal finance apps do one of two things: they're either too simple (just logging income and expenses) or so complex that you end up using only 10% of their features. Neither tells you what you actually need to know: where does your money quietly disappear?, how much of what you earn is genuinely free to invest or spend?, what would happen to your finances if one specific variable changed?

The underlying problem is that most of these tools are passive — they show you what already happened, but don't help you understand the pattern or project the future.

## The solution

Antigravity is a personal finance dashboard built around three concrete questions: how much do I have free (real cash flow)?, where is money silently leaking?, and what if...?

**Free Cash Flow**: not just income minus expenses, but real available income after fixed commitments, subscriptions, and recurring costs. The number that actually matters for making decisions.

**Leak Radar**: the most valuable module in the system. It detects invisible spending patterns — those subscriptions you forgot you had, recurring micro-expenses that accumulate, categories growing month over month below the radar. Gemini AI analyzes the transaction history and generates contextual suggestions explaining the detected pattern.

**Financial simulations**: "what if?" scenario modeling — what's the impact of increasing monthly savings by X, canceling a subscription, or changing a fixed expense threshold. Real-time visual projections.

**Reconciliation**: compares logged transactions against bank statements, automatically flagging discrepancies. The audit trail records every change with user, timestamp, and previous value for complete traceability.

Automatic synchronization via webhook lets transactions enter the system without manual friction. Multi-language support ensures the app works in the user's context, not the other way around.

## Stack and decisions

**React + TypeScript** for the frontend: the highly interactive nature of the dashboard — real-time charts, chained filters, simulations with immediate feedback — made a reactive framework the natural choice. TypeScript prevents the most painful classes of bugs when handling financial calculations.

**Supabase** as backend-as-a-service: PostgreSQL with Row Level Security for per-user data isolation, realtime subscriptions for dashboard sync without polling, and integrated authentication. The alternative (Firebase/Firestore) would have required less natural data structures for financial queries involving complex aggregations.

**Gemini AI** for contextual suggestions: the model analyzes the user's transaction history and generates readable insights — it doesn't just detect anomalies, it explains in natural language what pattern it found and why it's relevant.

**Vercel** for deployment: native Git workflow integration, edge functions for sync webhooks, and preview deployments for every PR.

## Features

- Transaction tracking with manual and automatic categorization
- Real Free Cash Flow (available income after fixed commitments)
- Leak Radar: money leak detection and forgotten subscription discovery
- Subscription management with renewal alerts
- Financial simulations with visual projections
- Reconciliation against bank statements
- Complete audit trail with change traceability
- Webhook for automatic transaction synchronization
- Multi-language support
- Contextual suggestions powered by Gemini AI

## Impact

- Real visibility into available cash flow, not just account balance
- Proactive detection of spending that goes unnoticed month after month
- Financial decisions based on data and projections, not gut feelings
- Complete traceability: every transaction and every change has a history
